Contributions

  • For p1_milestone, I was primarily responsible for managing health, pickups, the UI to display them, as well as godmode.
  • For p1_alpha, my main responsibility was setting up the game's progression. I was in charge of studying the original dungeon and then getting enemies to spawn in the right places when the player enters a room. To do this, I also needed to create a spawner entity that would handle the start and end of a room's combat. I also make the old man room and learned how to put canvases into the world in the process. Finally, I created Aquamentus as well as its fireballs.
  • p1_gold was originaly my idea, although there's no doubt that my teammates contributed immensely to coming up with its concept. I was largely the one in charge of creating the dash mechanic while my teammates worked on making the level it would be used in. I was also responsible for, the dash pickup, the stamina system, the new UI with the health bar and stamina bar, and I also used random websites to create custom NES-era sounds and sprites. I also came up with the idea of making green enemies, and I implemented a green stalfos, keese, and goriya as a proof of concept. Only the stalfos made it into the final build.
  • p1_gold began as a deranged 5-hour coding binge where I tried to turn our build of Zelda into one of my favorite games currently, ULTRAKILL. In that game the player dashes quickly with the use of a stamina system and kills enemies to increase their style rating. I implmented both the stamina-fueled dashes as well as the style system and created a testing room with Samus enemies to kill. My teammates weren't too impressed when I proposed that we could use this for our p1_gold build, and rightfully so. There wasn't a lot to do with the style mechanic, it mostly existed for its own sake. Instead, what they ended up enjoying was the dash, and it led to us eventually turning it into the entirety of our p1_gold. I'm glad I had my teammates to give me feedback as, if I did p1_gold alone, I probably would have done something silly with the style system after going through all the trouble of making it, rather than dropping it like I should have.
